Abby Larner: A Song Discovery

I was tasked with figuring out a song that expresses me. I'm of the belief that who I am is reflected not only in my own opinions of my self but also the opinions of those I interact with.

This song discovery project is my attempt to find a song that represents me by surveying people I interact with and choose a song that represents their views of me as well as my own.

I surveyed a group of 62 people that know me in a variety of capacities and for varying lengths of time. The average amount of time I have know one of the people I surveyed was 9 years and 9 months. The least amount of time I have known a person is 2 months and the most is 27 years.

Below you will find a summary of the data and my ultimate choice for a song that expresses me.

Songs by Release Date

Of the 62 songs, 16 were released before my birthday on July 27th, 1989. Below is a summary of the songs released during different periods of my life.

1792 - 1988: Pre-birth

Sixteen songs were released before my birth year in 1989.

1989 - 1993: Early Childhood

I was born on July 27th, 1989. Four songs were released during this period. Of note, Under the Sea from The Little Mermaid was released in 1989.

1994 - 1999: Elementary School

Seven songs were released during my elementary school years. During this period my family moved to Ohio and both The Distance by Cake and Go the Distance from Hercules were released.

2000 - 2003: Middle School

Ten of the 62 songs were released while I was in middle school. In 6th grade I played Macbeth in the school play and Survivor by Destiny's Child was released and won a Grammy for Best R&B performance by a Duo or Group with Vocals.

2004 - 2007: High School

Five songs were released while I was in high school. I played the flute in the marching band and trombone in the jazz band. I graduated in 2007 at the same time the Kanye West's album Graduation with song Stronger was released.

2008 - 2011: College

Six songs were released while I attended college at first at Haverford College and then at the University of Virginia. Two of the songs from the beginning and end of my college career, Diva and Run the World (Girls), were by Beyonce.

2012 - 2016: Professional Years

Eleven of the 62 songs were released during the 5 years after college. None of them were released in 2015, the year that I moved from Madison, Wisconsin to Austin, Texas.

Beats per Minute (BPM)

The beats per minute of a song represents how fast or slow the song is. The higher the number of beats per minute, the faster the song is. The average number of beats per minute for the 62 songs was 110. The lowest BPM of any song was 58 (We are the Champions, Queen) and the highest BPM was 180 (Eye of the Tiger, Survivor).

What's the song that expresses me?

Well, I was looking for a song that could hit the most representated aspects from each data point. I still needed to feel like the song represented me as well. Here's a summary of what I was looking for:

  • A pop song in the pop/rock category

  • An artist from the United States or one of the most represented artists: Beyonce or Queeen.

  • A release date before I was born or within the past five years.

  • A song with a bpm of about 110 or close to the most represented bpm, 128 (a fast-paced, up-tempo song).

  • A song with a mood that represents the most common mood throughout the selections. A few are confident, cheerful, amiable, playful, energetic, exhuberant, lively, celebratory, strong, and light.
